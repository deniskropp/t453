## Why D now

You already have the runtime, persistence, and a read-only monitor bridge; the missing piece is a first-class declarative surface that can emit or consume the same semantics. A KickLang export is the cleanest way to encode the contract as portable intent, especially since DSLs work best when they separate specification from execution and can describe event-driven behavior declaratively. It also keeps the bridge concept intact: the monitor is observational, not causal, so it maps naturally to declarative state and alert blocks. [cs.cmu](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~mmv/papers/02dars-monitoring.pdf)

## What the export should include

The minimal KickLang block should define:
- The `meta_dna` payload with version `1.2`.
- Typed event schemas for decision, metrics, memory_write, arbitration, and visual.
- Memory write policies: episodic append-only, semantic validated-merge, policy immutable-log.
- Runtime guards: scope, expiry, write_policy_enforcement, arbitration_required.
- Stage00 through Stage20 with the persistence and monitor hooks.
- A `monitor` section for flux, drift, valence, trend, and alerts derived from `metrics_event`.

That gives you a one-to-one declarative mirror of the TS runtime without introducing new behavior. [ceur-ws](https://ceur-ws.org/Vol-2504/paper28.pdf)

## What the DSL block should emphasize

The DSL should treat:
- `Stage10` as a typed decision emission point.
- `Stage16` as conflict-only arbitration.
- `Stage20` as the sole writer of memory and metrics events.
- `CoherenceMonitorBridge` as an observer over the metrics stream, not an owner of state.
- `convergence` as a deterministic predicate over the metrics log.

That structure matches how monitoring DSLs and event-calculus style runtimes separate observation from transition logic. [repository.upenn](https://repository.upenn.edu/server/api/core/bitstreams/70c17cfa-384a-4064-9db1-7a2e4e20ddf5/content)
